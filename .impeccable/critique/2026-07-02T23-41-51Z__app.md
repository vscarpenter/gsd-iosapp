## Anti-Patterns Verdict

Not slop — a genuinely designed system. Documented token spine (`Surface`/`Radius`/`QuadrantStyle`), serif rationed to display roles only (measured consistent across all 28 shots), four quadrant pigments carrying all strong color, dark mode re-derived rather than inverted, per-quadrant empty-state copy, Reduce-Motion-aware confetti. The deterministic track confirmed: no gradient text, no glassmorphism, radii match the token scale everywhere, no safe-area violations.

Pattern matches worth judgment: (a) every task card carries a 3pt quadrant side-stripe (`TaskCardView.swift:30`) — a banned pattern in the abstract, but here it's the functional category encoding shared with the web app; the real issue is triple-encoding (stripe + tinted chip + accent icons) on one card; (b) the dashboard leads with six big-number/small-label tiles — the hero-metric template in editorial clothing; (c) settings toggles render iOS-default green against the app's declared single blue tint — three different "on" colors on one screen.

## Cognitive Load (3/8 failures — moderate)

- **>4 options per decision point:** card `⋯` menu = 8 top-level items + two submenus (Snooze: 6, Move to: 4); editor = 12 sections in one scroll; iPad sidebar = 11 ungrouped smart-view rows.
- **Chunking:** same sidebar block and editor form lack second-level grouping.
- **Working-memory bridges:** "Blocked b…" truncation forces opening the task to learn what blocks it; capture shorthand must be memorized after onboarding.

## Emotional Journey

Peak 1: onboarding — the best surface in the app ("Yours, and only yours."). Valley 1: first capture — the resting capture bar shows a trash can + "Eliminate" (parser default quadrant), reading as "this input goes to the trash." Peak 2: completion — quadrant-pigment disc fill, haptic, on-palette confetti. Valley 2: opening a task — "Edit / Cancel … Save" pills, then 10 of 12 sections hidden below the medium detent; on iPad the quadrant picker is sliced mid-button by the sheet edge. End note: the dashboard closes sessions on the app's only filled-red element plus flame/trophy tiles — urgency and gamification, the two things PRODUCT.md swears off.

## What's Working

1. **Token discipline is real and load-bearing.** Warm-paper ramp, one action tint, four pigments — and the app obeys them; dark mode is a true re-mix (banner correctly re-derives to salmon/near-black; tag chips pass 4.70:1 dark).
2. **Action-surface parity architecture:** one `TaskRowMenu` feeds context menu, `⋯`, swipe actions, and VoiceOver custom actions — consistency most shipped apps fake.
3. **Efficiency ceiling worthy of Linear:** ⌘K everywhere, ⌘1-4, full-swipe complete with directional-lock on iPad, bulk select, widgets/intents/Spotlight.

## Priority Issues

1. **[P1] The task editor is the app's weakest moment — and broken on iPad.** `.presentationDetents([.medium, .large])` applies unconditionally (`TaskEditorView.swift:117`): on iPad the sheet cuts the quadrant picker's second row mid-tile (measured 27pt of tile then hard clip, no scroll indicator — ipad-05/06/07/08/13); on iPhone the medium detent hides subtasks/due/dependencies with no affordance that they exist. A stray `EditButton()` at `.topBarLeading` (`:105`) renders "Edit" inside "Edit Task" (and inside "New Task"). Fix: large-only (or no detents) on regular size class; delete the toolbar EditButton, scope reordering to the Subtasks section; add a scroll hint or section index at medium detent.
2. **[P1] Muted text fails the app's own AA contract, system-wide.** `ink3` (0xA49B8D light / 0x6F685B dark, `Theme.swift:42`) carries real content at body size: due dates 2.74:1, sidebar counts 2.61:1, quadrant counts 2.43:1, dashboard labels/axes ~2.7:1 light, ~3.0:1 dark — all below the 4.5:1 floor PRODUCT.md commits to. Blocked-card dimming stacks on top: metadata at 1.79:1. Also: completion-ring stroke 1.56:1 (WCAG 1.4.11 wants ≥3:1), capture placeholder 1.74:1. Fix: darken `ink3` toward `ink2`, exempt nothing that conveys state; replace opacity-dimming of blocked cards with a lock badge + full-contrast text.
3. **[P1] The dashboard contradicts both the brand and itself.** Flame Streak + trophy Best-streak tiles and a full-width filled `Surface.alert` banner — the loudest element in the app — front a product whose brief bans streaks and urgency-bait (`DashboardView.swift:72-89`). And the By-Quadrant donut's center says "10 active" while its legend sums 24 (it charts active+completed) — a trust-eroding data mismatch on the numbers screen. Fix: restyle the banner as an alert-wash card with rust text linking to Overdue Backlog; reframe streak tiles as neutral consistency stats without flame/trophy; make the donut chart the same population its center counts.
4. **[P2] Destructive delete has no guard and no undo.** `TaskActions.delete` commits immediately from swipe, `⋯`, and VoiceOver action (`TaskActions.swift:32-36`). For an offline-first app, one mis-swipe is unrecoverable. Fix: a 5-second "Deleted — Undo" capsule (calmer than a confirm dialog); the tombstone design makes soft-undo feasible.
5. **[P2] The capture bar sabotages its own first impression.** Resting state shows trash + "Eliminate"; the placeholder that teaches the syntax truncates at default size ("(try !! * #t…") and measures 1.74:1; at AX sizes the chip hyphenates ("Elimi-nate") and eats half the bar. On iPhone the whole bar sits in the worst thumb zone (top `safeAreaInset`). Fix: neutral "Auto" chip until text is typed; shorten placeholder; icon-only chip at AX sizes; consider a bottom capture affordance on iPhone.

## Persona Red Flags

**Alex (power user):** quadrant override is a blind 5-state cycle (3 taps to reach Delegate); palette matching is substring-only — typos return nothing; multi-select hides behind an unlabeled "Edit" pill; no in-app shortcut reference outside the Mac menu.

**Sam (accessibility):** AX editor keeps a 2-column quadrant grid → "Sched-ule" hyphenation, tile tops misaligned by ~17pt, bottom buttons clipped (iphone-21); "1 day a…" truncation loses the overdue meaning; tag-remove × is 28pt, card `⋯` is 30pt — both under 44pt; iPad `SwipeRevealRow` gestures need a VoiceOver-equivalent audit. Strong: combined card labels, 44pt disc wrapper, Reduce-Motion suppression.

**Casey (one-handed, distracted):** capture — the marquee act — is at the top of the iPhone screen with no bottom entry; reaching Eliminate means scrolling three sections; the post-typing "Details" affordance is footnote-sized. Good: full-swipe complete, floating tab bar, medium-detent editor controls in the thumb zone.

**Priya (privacy-conscious, deciding on sync):** exemplary copy overall, but "Fetch titles for shared links" — the app's only silent network call — defaults ON (opt-out, off-key for "privacy is the product"), and sign-in rows give no preview of what sync entails before the OAuth bounce.

## Minor Observations

- iPad matrix truncates card titles to 1 line at ~464pt width ("Send the weekly ne…") while siblings wrap to 2 — allow 2 lines before ellipsis; ~32% dead paper below the grid in portrait.
- "GSD - Matrix" literal hyphen in the branded title — an en-dash/middot fits the editorial register; it also duplicates the tab label.
- Sign-in card: filled black Apple button between two text links (SIWA wants parity, not dominance).
- Settings toggles are iOS-default green vs the declared blue tint; third "on" color on the screen.
- Onboarding page 1's button says "Get started" but just advances to page 2; iPad onboarding floats tiny in the 13" canvas with a full-width pill.
- "Search" magnifier opens a sheet titled "Commands" — pick one noun.
- "Tracked < 1m" stat needs a zero-state ("Nothing tracked yet").
- Dashed "Created" trend line ≈0.5pt at 2.6:1 — nearly invisible in both modes.
- An open editor sheet survives deep-link tab switches (protective for edits, but it can strand a stale sheet over unrelated screens).
- Demo data: created-line spikes to ~24 on one day — makes the marketing screenshots read as a backlog explosion.

## Questions to Consider

3. The medium detent hides 10 of the editor's 12 sections — progressive disclosure or a locked basement? Where's the one hint that the product's depth (dependencies, recurrence, time tracking) exists?
